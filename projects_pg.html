CilantroAudit

Scope

This project was sponsored by a man named Todd, who is the manager at International Paper Specialty Ink and Plate Division (Beaverton, OR). The issue: his company's auditing practices were very outdated. All audits across all departments were still done in paper. The focus for our project was their housekeeping department, where an inability to keep track of issues via audits could lead to a potentially dangerous environment. For example, if an Automatic Electronic Defribrillator (AED) was missing signage, causing it to be unfound, and someone was having a medical emergency, then the missing signage could very well be the difference between life and death.

Our Solution

CilantroAudit is a cross-platform application that allows admins to create new audits, which users can fill out and send to the database. Both admins and users can then quickly retrieve complete audits from the database to view, as well export to PDF or Excel. Our software also insures that multiple employees won't complete the same exact audit via a lockout feature, and allowed admins to track flagged-issue trends over time - making it much easier to find urgent problems that need resolution. Our number-one focus for UI development was ease-of-use, because the target audience was a less technologically-savvy demographic.

Dev Team

In this project, I worked with a team of 8 other capstone students following an agile development model. We maintained modern coding practices, followed the PEP-8 Python coding standard, the builder OO pattern, and implemented well-documented and well-tested code. The result: a maintanable, expansible software solution.
